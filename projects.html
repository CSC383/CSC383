Regional Asset Mapping

  • UM-Flint’s EDA University Center will house the I69 Thumb Region Comprehensive Economic Development Asset Mapping Initiative. We will identify regional assets, evaluate each asset’s impact, collect data, conduct an annual regional business climate survey, consolidate findings, and create and disseminate the asset map. UM-Flint student research assistants will assist at every stage. This asset mapping initiative addresses the Region’s distinct need and desire to develop a systematic data collection platform to better inform economic development planning and decision-making and evaluate the impact of economic development programs.

Faculty/Student Research tab

  • UM-Flint is establishing a dedicated EDA University Center Research Fund to support faculty/student teams in applied research that addresses the region’s economic and business development needs. University research teams will work side-by-side with community groups, enhancing learning for students while increasing the capacity of the community. Research stakeholders will range from businesses, non-profits, and community organizations to local government, hospitals, and economic development entities. When appropriate, the Center will leverage expertise from UM-Ann Arbor, as well as other university partners in the state.

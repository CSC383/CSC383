About Us

The overall goal of the newly established UM-Flint EDA University Center for Community and Economic Development is to coordinate, inform, and contribute to economic development efforts that cultivate innovation and advance high-growth entrepreneurship needed to build and sustain a diversified economy throughout the 7-county I-69 Thumb Region (Genesee, Shiawassee, Lapeer, St. Clair, Huron, Sanilac, and Tuscola counties).

The EDA University Center will receive direction and guidance from stakeholders who rely on its services and research, and a regional advisory board, consisting of a representative cross-section of the I69 counties.

This website/portal serves the following purposes:

  • Disseminate UM-Flint EDA University Center research information
  • Facilitate access to regional economic data (for existing businesses, entrepreneurs, researchers, developers, and site selectors)
  • Offer entrepreneurs and existing businesses virtual access to UM-Flint EDA University Center Resources
  • Identify current service providers, their locations, services, and links to their websites on an interactive map
